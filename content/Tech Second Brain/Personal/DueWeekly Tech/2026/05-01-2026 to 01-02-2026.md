# Architecture

[1. Platform Engineer - How to set up an Internal Developer Platform: An implementation guide](https://platformengineering.org/blog/how-to-set-up-an-internal-developer-platform) 🌟 **(Recommended)**

- This article provides a comprehensive, phase-by-phase guide to building an Internal Developer Platform (IDP), offering a complete strategy for designing your own. The author emphasizes that choosing the wrong architectural direction can cause significant disruption for platform engineers building complex systems; exploring this article will help you understand and avoid these common pitfalls.
- You will find several MVPs (Minimum Viable Products) that demonstrate how IDPs are implemented in the industry by following the [IDP Golden Path](https://platformengineering.org/blog/what-are-golden-paths-a-guide-to-streamlining-developer-workflows). These MVPs serve as concrete evidence to prove whether your platform strategy is effective. An IDP MVP typically evolves through four key phases: Discovery, Integration, Deployment, and Adoption Planning.

[2. Youtube - From Zero to Fully Operational Developer Platform in 5 Steps!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fZ2DjdqT1e0) 🌟 **(Recommended)**

- This insightful video by [Viktor Farcic](https://www.linkedin.com/in/viktorfarcic/) provides a comprehensive guide to building an Internal Development Platform (IDP) from scratch. While specific tech stacks may vary, the video covers the essential fundamentals required to define and execute your IDP strategy.
- Farcic outlines four mandatory implementation pillars: the **API Layer** (Kubernetes API or a custom Platform API), **State Management** (Git), **CI/CD Actions** (e.g., GitHub Actions), and **Governance** (RBAC and Policies). 
- Furthermore, he explores advanced components to enhance the developer experience (DevEx) and platform maturity, such as **CLIs**, **GitOps** workflows, and optional **Web UIs**. If you are looking to architect a robust IDP, this video offers significant value in defining your roadmap.

[3. Medium - 10 Secure Secrets Patterns: KMS, SOPS, and Vault](https://medium.com/@sparknp1/10-secure-secrets-patterns-kms-sops-and-vault-9cdd51ab29a6) 🌟 **(Recommended)**

- These insightful articles provide essential information for implementing robust security patterns. In my experience, these tech stacks remain the gold standard, and they should be considered foundational strategies for any DevOps or AWS Engineer.
- Throughout the content, you will explore best practices for secret management, patterns for securing source code, and methods for protecting applications against common security risks. It also outlines workflows to integrate these beyond-standard strategies into your pipeline. From my perspective, these articles demonstrate the ideal mindset and architectural approach for anyone looking to harden their application security.

[4. Medium - How to Make Architecture Decisions: RFCs, ADRs, and Getting Everyone Aligned](https://medium.com/@lukasniessen/how-to-make-architecture-decisions-rfcs-adrs-and-getting-everyone-aligned-ab82e5384d2f) 🌟 **(Recommended)**

- This article outlines a highly effective mindset for those seeking to make informed architectural choices and convince stakeholders of their validity. It provides a step-by-step guide to documenting these decisions using **RFCs (Request for Comments) and ADRs (Architecture Decision Records).**
- Implementing this process can create a significant impact across an organization when introducing brand-new features. It encourages early involvement from relevant experts and interested stakeholders, allowing them to review and contribute to the documentation before a formal meeting occurs. The article provides templates to help you implement and deliver professional RFC documentation, empowering you to lead this process effectively.
- Furthermore, the content details the entire lifecycle of a decision—from the initial comment phase to the final meeting—concluding with the creation of an ADR. By learning from common pitfalls highlighted in these articles, you can refine your own strategies, which is especially valuable for teams or organizations that currently lack a formal decision-making framework.

# Technology

[1. Youtube - Top 10 DevOps & AI Tools You MUST Use in 2026](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=65o_j4E7_lk)

- This insightful video highlights the trending technologies for 2026. A huge shout-out to [Viktor Farcic](https://www.linkedin.com/in/viktorfarcic/) for creating this content; his expertise provides high-value validation for those looking to adopt emerging tech. The video covers essential tools for building and deploying AI, including AI Agents, automated Code Review, LLMs, and Vector Databases. Additionally, it explores the Kubernetes and Cloud Native ecosystem, focusing on local development environments, Internal Developer Platforms (IDPs), platform testing, and modern infrastructure scripting.
- If you are looking to expand your knowledge of Cloud Native ecosystems and AI-driven DevOps, I highly recommend watching this video and exploring his other content. It offers excellent insights for brainstorming new strategic approaches to modern platform engineering.

[2. Medium - Bastion Host for Cloud & On-Premise](https://medium.com/@alokpabalkar/bastion-host-for-cloud-on-premise-8a5711298d24) 🌟 **(Recommended)**

- This tutorial demonstrates how to implement a bastion host or jump box to secure connections to critical resources such as databases, cloud storage, and other sensitive assets. This approach is a standard, robust solution for mitigating and preventing unauthorized access. The tutorial provides compatible strategies for implementing these hosts using `docker` across both Cloud and On-Premises environments.
- Additionally, the article covers User Access Control (UAC) management, which facilitates team collaboration by defining specific user permissions for host access. This implementation is straightforward and ensures a high success rate for secure connectivity. 	- [Medium - Creating a Bastion Host for Secure Access to Your AWS Infrastructure with Terraform](https://towardsaws.com/creating-a-bastion-host-for-secure-access-to-your-aws-infrastructure-with-terraform-17ee287bb3d)
	- [Medium - Securing Data with a Bastion Host with Terraform in AWS](https://collin-smith.medium.com/securing-data-with-a-bastion-host-with-terraform-in-aws-ce88c4e7b32c)
